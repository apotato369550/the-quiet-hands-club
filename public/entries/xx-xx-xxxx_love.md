Love.

My answer to the age-old question.

Author's note: 

I wrote this essay shortly after I got rejected from Valentines day. It's currently mid-March of 2026. I recently had my first kiss, but sadly it didn't feel like it came from a place of love.

During the leadup to Valentines' I sent a letter to a boy that I found kinda cute, but learned that he was seeing someone else. Weirdly enough, when you've had your heart broken enough times and your foot crushed in every attempt to enter the door, it doesn't hurt as much. Painful, yes - but a lot easier to shrug and dust yourself off. Having a mood disorder and being a hopeless romantic is one hell of a cocktail. 

Enough talk. Essay time!

---

2/12/2026

Today I'll be trying my best - the best that I can at the moment - to give an answer to the age-old question about love. As Valentines day slowly creeps closer and closer, it seems more likely that I'll be spending it without a partner. Naturally, this is where people say that they're probably gonna spend it alone, but to me, I think that's the furthest from the truth.

Despite never being in a romantic relationship, I've had my heart shattered so many times by people unable to reciprocate in any meaningful way. My foot being crushed by the door in every attempt to try and walk in. It could have been nice. It could have been really nice. Some things just aren't meant to be [sadly].

The hardest part is when odds and biology are against you - and probably [maybe] society. When what you perceive, feel, and view as love gets so violently shunned by everyone else, it's hard not to be resentful. It's difficult not to despair. And it makes it so easy for me to forget the growth and beauty that came each time I let the roses decay.

In a game where the odds are always against you, and no matter what move you make, playing or not playing, either way you will have wasted time, the best move in any case is to play it with all your being. Not because you should. Not because you must. But because in all ways, it's the best thing you can do. Period. No one else can play it for you. The worse fate is to wake up at 80 and regret not playing more dice.

If only people understood that I can never be attracted to a woman the same way I am attracted to a man. If only people understood the parts of me that had to grow while pushing back hard at the inevitability of loss, rejection, and heartbreak. And I wish people understood the struggle to balance that while having faith, when the people who wield 'faith' wield it against you. If not explicitly, then implicitly. If not implicitly, then through doubt, despair, and struggle in balancing the contradiction in one's mind.

I am happy. I am grateful that despite every disappointment, I have recovered and continued to pursue love. In all areas except the one I currently mourn, I've known it. I've felt it. I've received it. And I've done my best to give it. It's the greatest feeling of all. It's the greatest act of all.

The hours sunk into a craft that go by like it's been minutes. Group chats filled to the brim with funny messages. Eating with colleagues and coworkers. Good food shared between family. And realizing that when you kneel in front of the altar that what you've been looking for all this time was right in front of you. What you've been hoping to receive you've been trying to give so freely and readily. And that when it does come in time to receive it, it'll be both beautiful and bittersweet. Transient, but neverending. As long as you believe that there is more to be given and to be received.

And above all, it waits so patiently to be given and to be discovered by all those who choose it willingly.

With love,

The Steward.
