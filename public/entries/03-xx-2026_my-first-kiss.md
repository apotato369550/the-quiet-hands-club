My First Kiss

A few days before I held that donation drive, where we went to a school in Guadalupe to teach kids there Scratch, I gave my first kiss to my first love. Someone who couldn't return it fully. Someone who - on paper, didn't deserve it. Someone who you'd consider to be seemingly unlovable. I gave it, and it was one of the happiest moments of my life.

It was two days before our scheduled outreach date. I met up with the guy a few days prior in one of the computer labs of our department. I saw him, we weren't exactly on good terms before - because he had rejected me the Christmas prior. I couldn't blame him, I just wish he had been a bit gentler.

That night, the both of use leaning on my family's red Hyundai Getz, which was as old as me, reminiscing and recounting life since we first met. We've both gotten really far in life. Him and his mental health, me and managing stress due to org work, school, and actual work.

It was just the two of us. My heart was racing because I knew in that moment, that it was the last time things were seemingly normal. 

For Christmas I gave him a book, and a letter by me, revealing my true feelings for him.

The night I received that text message: "ily no homo" was the moment my heart shatterned into bits, for the umpteenth time. I clutched my rosary while uttering Hail Mary's, hoping it would somehow dull the pain.

That morning, we had another outreach planned - we went to San Jose and taught kids programming there as well - and I had met up with one of his close friends, a girl who was the assigned photographer. I revealed to her my plan, and she asked me one crucial detail: "Will you be ok?"

I said: "Yes."

I had been through so much worse before. Not to brag, or to compare, the last time I had feelings that strong I went insane. In the literal sense. I was never the same after that, and I was never the same after this happened.

That night, I jokingly asked him: "Where's my kiss?" - and he gave me one. Not a real one, just cheek to cheek. And I gave him one back, my right hand covering his lips so as to not make it official. I bid him goodbye as he rode his Maxim (or was it a MoveIt?) home. He had an assignment due that night, and that letter might have fucked him up. My bad.

Anyways, back to the computer lab. I did a bold thing and I sat next to him. I pretended - tried- to do some actual work, but I kept glancing at him. He looked stressed. Scared, even. Like how I was a reminder of what he failed to break gently. I grabbed his forearm and squeezed. A close friend of mine did that for me whenever I had anxiety attacks. I looked at him and asked: "Is everything ok?" and he nodded.

We made some small talk and we went back to doing what we were doing prior. I texted him. I told him that I was ready to talk about everything.

It was 7:30 - an hour before my usual 8:30 scrum. I didn't have much time, there were too many things to talk about, and he was the type of person who was late for everything and had a habit of rescheduling. I rode the elevator downstairs and wore my signature brown jacket that I've worn since I was in 8th grade. I'd like to think that everyone at school knew me by it. It had a button missing on one of it's sleeves (I can't remember if it was the left one or the right one).

I met up with him at the mall's Uniqlo. I asked if he had eaten yet, and he said no. I was the type to spoil him, much to my "detriment". I didn't feel the need to, he was just someone I cared about who didn't know how to take care of himself properly. Money wasn't an issue, but I needed to find somewhere quiet.

We both wandered through the park at night, my nervousness slowly fading, replacing itself with familiarity. We were trying to find someplace nice to eat and we landed at a Yellow Cab. We both ordered the same thing - carbonara with chicken strips and bread. It was so overpriced lmao. 300+?? Are you kidding me? Multiply that by two, and that's what I had to pay.

But. Having been scammed like that paled in comparison to what he dealt to me as soon as we sat down. I looked at his earrings. They were purple. We met at the gym the day before, and I was so nervous I acted weird around him. He looked so cute, bu that mixed with pain? Not ideal.

He had slept with someone.

I had prepared for this. The day before, I scribbled the things that I wanted to say. And here they were:

"I don't care if you find someone else."

"I don't care if we both graduate and never get to see each other again."

"I don't care if you have a kid with someone."

"I don't need you to be happy."

"I don't want use to be friends anymore."

"Even if what we had was friendship, even if you took my feelings out of the equation, it was totally lopsided."

"Friends don't do that with each other. My friends don't do that."

"Despite all that, I want you to stay."

A few things I'd also like to add, friends don't say "I love you" to each other so casually. Friends don't let each other get so physically close that they can hear each other's heartbeat. Friends don't tell each other about the deepest, darkest, almost shameful parts of ourselves to others. Go on movie dates: Wicked 2, FNAF, 28 Years Later. Attend mass together.

He didn't know what to say. He looked so confused. So guilty. It was nothing he could have expected. We couldn't even look each other in the eye. I lost my appetite. He started asking questions.

"If we aren't friends, then what are we?"

"I don't know."

"If I give you a kiss, will it give you closure?"

"No. You'd have to want it."

I told him that I would always look out for him, no matter what. One way or another. About the struggles I've had to face ever since becoming president of my org. The eyes, the whispers, the voices, the rumors, the drama. My heart. I've had to take meds for that as well.

He spilled his drink, I wiped the table clean. He was still hungry after he finished more than half of his food. I offered him mine to no objection.

I asked if we could walk somewhere.

Before we arrived at the Yellow Cab, he got a phone from his mom. I've met her before. She's a very nice woman. A professor in a state uni. He told me that he used to grade her papers for her during her free time, and that one of the things he wanted to be after he graduated was a prof. We met each other during his first psychiatrist appointment (we share the same psychiatrist).

It was a rainy afternoon, and we booked a grab. I was in uniform, I was waiting for him outside the dormitory complex of our campus. I was holding an umbrella while waiting for him. He booked a grab. We split the cost between us, even though I offered to pay it for him (haha. simp.). 

I think. That ride was when I realized that I was really in love with him. We were nearing the overpass that led to Ayala when he asked me if I could wake him up from his nap. I told him yes.

He had his eyes closed, and I had to restrain myself from staring at him. His head leaned against the raindrop-stained car-window. It was cold because of the aircon, but my heart was on fire. Behold, the most beautiful man I've ever laid eyes on - at least that's what it felt like in that moment (might have been a bit of an exaggeration). He looked so vulnerable. It felt like I was intruding lol.

It wasn't the first time we've shared rides together. Jeeps, buses - there were times we were almost forced next too each other because it was so crammed.

I remembered why I withstood everything. The uncertainty, the pain, the pressure, the heat. Not just from me. He also felt things deeply, so much so, to the point where he hurts himself intentionally. The weight of that secret - and of another - his diagnosis - ate me alive for the better part of a year.

We walked to the part of the park with all the lights. Next to the terminal. And for some reason, I asked him: 

"Could I give you something?"

"What?"

"A kiss?"

He looked at me with a stare that read: "This guyyyyy...". We were about to do it. He agreed, although implicitly. We were trying to find a spot.

I looked to the tree with all the lights at the center of the park, but we both agreed it was too public - and he was getting impatient.

I look to my left and found a dark, almost secluded area, next to the abandoned pyramid-shaped building which used to house a restaurant (I've eaten there. It was mid). There was a street lamp. It was dark lol. This part, we argued, was the... part where it happened. I'll paraphrase because it was a doozy.

"XXXX, come on!?"

"Are you sure?"

And then he grabbed my face and kissed me. It felt more like a headbutt, really. I laughed my ass off and shortly returned the favor. The rest of the time, it was just us walking back to my office. It was almost time - I had 10 minutes left before my meeting. I kept making sex jokes - which made all the sex jokes he made about me - turn sour like milk. I kept "asking" if he was a top or a bottom (he was obv. a bottom): a giver or a taker. LMAOOOOOO.

In hindsight, I shouldn't have said that. I was high on the fact that I finally got what I wanted at 21. A kiss. But nothing more.

He wanted us to go our separate ways. He implied that it was me who wanted to kiss him. And after that, silence.

There was this one occasion where I was half-drunk at a karaoke bar where the weight of what happened - or what he did to me - or whatever finally sunk in. I couldn't contain it anymore. I felt really guilty afterwards, but it was eating me alive almost.

That, and all the time I so happily wasted with him, the meals that we shared, the time we spent studying and talking about all sorts of stuff and about how his ex left him with so much trauma (daw). WOW!

If this isn't the funniest shit ever, I don't know what is. You'd need full context of my life in order to see how I find it funny. Or maybe I am just crazy.

I wish love were easy.

The Steward.
