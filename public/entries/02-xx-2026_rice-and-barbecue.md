Rice and Barbeque

Some time last month, I gave a young girl my rice and barbeque.

I was commuting to work from the gym. Before I hopped on the jeepney, I stopped by a local karenderia near my university (the gym was close to where I studied) and bought some sticks of barbecue and a cup of rice. I knew I was going to be hungry.

I walked my normal route to our office building, and along the way I saw a girl sitting down on the sidewalk, just ahead of the guy and his motor where I normally buy malunggay pandesal from. I walked past her initially, and I went up to my office building.

For some reason, I can't remember fully, our friends decided it was a good idea to eat out later. It was around 5-6pm so it was relatively early. I wanted to join them - and I also thought that eating too much food wasn't a good idea. Although I came from the gym, I know what it's like to be too full and have too much.

You feel icky. You feel guilty. And most distressing of all would be the knowledge that if you had been more patient, and if you had eaten at a time you were actually hungry, and ideally around better company, you would have enjoyed the food more.

That girl was alone and hungry. I had the food. I didn't mind giving it away.

I took the elevator down and I walked back along my route, going across the road to the Dunkin, then past the kuya selling pandesal. She looked at me so expectantly. She was hungry. It seemed all she could think of was getting a bite of food.

She gestured at the plastic bag I carried with the barbecue and the rice, and she gestured to her mouth. I smiled warmly and gave it to her. I looked back and went to my office again.

There was a separate occasion where I bought another serving of rice and barbecue and went to that exact same spot, hoping to find her again, but she was no longer there. I hope she found something to eat. I hope she is doing well. I hope she and her family is safe.

I don't know.

The Steward.
