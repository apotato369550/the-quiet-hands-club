# Introduction: Welcome to the Quiet Hands Club

"But when you do a charitable deed, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing, that your charitable deed may be in secret;

and your father who sees in secret, will Himself reward you openly"

That verse, mixed with some midnight inspiration, coupled with a cup of coffee, brought about the birth of The Quiet Hands Club. Also because I was in a bit of a LOTM (Lord of the Mysteries) rabbithole and I fell in love with the concept of a secret club (The Tarot Club) doing things to influence events in their reality.

Hi. You may call me The Steward. No real names exist, only titles and deeds. I started this club because I love helping people, but it's hard helping people when you don't know where the act actually comes from.

As someone who comes from a country where every good deed is coupled with a face, a name, or an institution - paired with pictures, testimonials and the like - it's hard to tell who's genuine anymore. That makes me sad.

I'm a shy person. I do a lot of things. If you ask me what I did for a living, I'd tell them I'm a student. We all are. We are all constantly learning.

But if only we learned what it meant to be good when no one is looking.

When we think of charity and generosity, or how things 'happen' in general, we think in terms of cause and effect. Or, if you're a CS/Math student:

Input -> Process -> Output

Or in math terms:

x -> f(x) -> y

Cause -> Effect

But to me, this denies the ultimate reality that:

Good things happen to bad people.

Bad things happen to good people.

All for no discernable reason.

I like to think that miracles happen because a bunch of good people - their names redacted - were kind and humble enough to part ways with their bread and fish. That there was an extra shipment of wine, that just so happened to appear, because a good man really needed it, and because it was of no expense to the one giving it.

That people keep things ambigious. Obfuscate, omit details, and make things up - to keep the myth of someone more than a man alive - to have someone to look up to and imitate.

This line of thinking can't explain every miracle. Administration, logistics, money, and luck have their limits. But there is no limit to genuine human kindness.

This is the thing I wish to keep alive. The thing I wish to foster and let grow through this platform. So that one day, when my legs are too weak, eyes too dim, mouth too dry, and hands to frail to keep typing, I'll know I have done my part regardless.

It's a huge world out there. There is much to be done.

Look to hear more from me soon.

With love,

The Steward
