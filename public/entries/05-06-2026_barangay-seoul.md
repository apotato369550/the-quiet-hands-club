Barangay Seoul

Last night, I ate at a Barangay Seoul, and tipped the people there 50 Pesos (roughly a dollar).

I had just come from praying at a Chapel. How did I get there? I had no idea. I arrived at a Church near the place that I work which had a fitness suplements store next to it. I bought a month's worth of creatine, and for some reason, my feet decided to roam around. 

I entered the church through one of its side entrances. The people were standing. Some of them looked at me funny, but resumed with their devotion. It was nearly 5pm, and mass was about to end.

After the priest gave his final blessing, I went to their chapel. They were blessing the chapel and the Eucharist.

It was an out of body experience. I was surrounded by women 3 times older than me. I felt pressured to stay when they offered me to kneel in one of the front pews. They sang lyrics to a holy song I couldn't follow - and I'm normally great at pattern recognition. I love adlibbing harmonies to songs that are in my vocal range.

I was in a trance. I took off my shoes and my feet stunk and I felt really guilty. And by the time that it ended, one of the altar servers snapped me out of it because they were about to shut off the lights for good. 

I was a bit embarrassed when I left it. I was thinking "What the fuck was that??" as I went to grab dinner at the Barangay Seoul for work. Also, before the incident, I dropped a hundred in one of the donation boxes.

Weird. Memorable. Reminded me of when I was still in junior high. I was put in a Catholic school and we were forced (kinda against our will) to memorize worship songs, the responses and gestures during mass, and Catholic dogma. What a bad way to get someone into Christanity, especially someone who's naturally gonna question things.

I like the message, I did not like the messenger. All I remember were out-of-touch super-rich classmates, caring (but not really) teachers, sleep deprivation, trauma, neglect, and all that jazz. I've made peace with most of it, but it's a shame looking back. I wonder things would have turned out differently if my environment weren't as shitty and if they were supportive in a way that mattered, or if things at home were better.

Anyways, 

The Steward, signing off.
