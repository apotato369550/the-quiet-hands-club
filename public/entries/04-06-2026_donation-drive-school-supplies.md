Catch Codey

Last March 14th, I had the pleasure of holding both a donation drive and a coding camp at a public school somewhere in barangay Guadalupe.

Having arranged all of that had to be one of the most demanding and difficult things I've ever gone through in my life - but it had to be the most rewarding things I've ever done. There were a shitton of challenges, and because of it, I have a far larger respect for bureaucrats, paperworks, and event managers because of it. Sadly, I've lost some things in the process.

During on of the filings I got into a heated exchange with a friend (who's I don't consider a friend anymore) regarding my perceived lack of friends. It hurt because somewhere deep down, it felt true. Shockingly and undeniably true. I had always been an outcast. I never had a stable group of friends who understood me, or why I do the things that I do, or how I feel. And I've made peace with that. It's a shame that he said that. I thought he was a really chill guy. I got hurt, no one took accountability, and it really threw me off. I had no choice but to swallow it and carry it with me - and I still do to this day.

There were several misfilings during the leadup to the event, and I had to plead with the student affairs officer to let me file a separate activity, because it wasn't submitted properly. I felt like I was truly being judged by the one up above when I did, because when I showed up and was truly being honest about how I mishandled everything, I was shown mercy. I'll never forget it. I repaid their kindness with donuts and juice after our event concluded.

During the 2 week leadup to the event, our organization held a donation drive. We set up a booth in the canteen and had volunteers take care of it. I couldn't dedicate the time as much to taking care of the booth, because I was looking for sponsors and setting things up in the background - coordinating with our beneficiary school, and contacting our city's office for education. 

I wish I had the time to be present. I bought them donuts, I can't remember how many dozen nor how many times, but I think it made them happy. If only I found the time to enjoy it with them.

Thanks to our efforts we were able to donate and turn over 144 plastic envelopes full of school supplies: ballpens, pencils, notebooks, etc. to our partnered school. We had an amazing event as well - I was one of the main tutors/speakers for it. I hope they all learned something. They were 8th/9th grade students, but our lesson plan was Scratch. I hope at least a couple of them consider taking up CS or IT.

I know for certain that if I was around their age, and someone visited our school to show us robots or to program cool stuff, I'd be really happy.

Regards,

The Steward.
