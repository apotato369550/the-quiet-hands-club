Coffee Errands, and Being Late to Class

Some time this February, I gave bread, a jar of Stick-O, some rice, and some barbeque to some mendicants by the road, while grabbing coffee - being late to my data analytics class.

I always have this habit every Monday and Wednesday afternoon, during our Data Analytics class, to go out and buy coffee and food. I wish I had actual vacant time to dedicate towards eating and relaxing, but I didn't really have a choice. 5 straght subjects, from 9 AM to 4:30 PM. I'd get hungry after our advance calculus class and make my way downstairs just before the start of our next one.

Thankfully, our data analytics prof is very considerate haha. I'm pretty sure he's gay. He never pointed out the fact that I kept bringing food and coffee to my table, or I'd always show up late, hot, and sweaty - because summer in the Phillippines can get quite humid and whatnot. He always kept silent and always continued teaching... data science.

I had a very big thing for data science at one point. I was a DataCamp scholar, I currently am a DataCamp scholar. At one point I was in charge of 2 different DataCamp initiatives for two different orgs. I mainly focused on Python, and I'm very out of practice. I completed all the data science exercises in DataCamp - their Datalab notebooks - at least the ones that were in Python. And I really did my best not to AI all of them, and I really did my best to comprehend all of it, but like, I had to consult ChatGPT a lot of the time - especially in regards to the syntax. DataFrames amirite? Scaling, cleaning, PCA, clustering, regression, decision trees, random forests, yep. I made the leaderboards very consistently.

He taught it in R and used a really funny editor and we had to learn R markdown. I will admit I did none of the assignments myself HAHAHAHAHAHA. I relied a lot on my groupmates and on my one close friend who's also my co-worker for work. I'd pretend to do the task/assignment, and fail miserably because of the shitty wifi (and because my laptop is old, and I use Linux which adds another layer of complexity/difficulty. lmao)

The jar of Stick-O came from our algorithms and complexity class. I can't remember what group presented but they did give away prizes. I answered one of their questions and they gave me a jar of Stick-Os. I wasn't really hungry during that time. I wanted to give it away, but something told me to keep it for a bit. And later that afternoon I understood why.

When I went downstairs from our main building to go grab coffee from this nice pink coffee cart that I've started going to (I like visiting different coffee stands in and around campus), and as I walked my usual route, to my right was a mother and her child. They hidden away, behind some plants, trying to get shade from the scorching sun. Meanwhile, I was wearing my usual jacket (I always wear a jacket, even when it's hot). There was a Julie's bakeshop on the route to the coffee shop, so I bought some bread together with my coffee. The bread wasn't for me, it was for them.

When I gave it to them I had remembered the Stick-O that I won that morning. The child wasn't there - I was worried. I gave it to what I presumed to be her mom.

Something similar happened that week (if my memory serves me right) when I decided to give some rice and barbeque to some other dwellers. Actually, these were two separate events. I can't remember if I gave the mother and child bread, or rice and barbeque. But at this point, I don't think it matters too much.

I hope the child was happy when they returned to their mom.

With treats,

The Steward.
